=======================================================
  PHASE 2.3 — ALGORITHM COMPARISON
  LMS vs RLS vs Phase 1 Fixed Beamformer
=======================================================

=======================================================
  COMPARISON SUMMARY
=======================================================

Signal environment: M=8, theta_sig=30 deg, theta_int=-20 deg
SNR=10 dB, SIR=0 dB, N=512 samples

Algorithm                         SINR (dB)         Conv. sample     MACs/sample
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Phase 1 Fixed                         15.78                  N/A        O(M) = 8
LMS (mu=0.001)                        18.93                   15       O(M) = 32
RLS (lambda=1.000)                    18.90                    2    O(M^2) = 288
--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SINR improvement over fixed:
  LMS: +3.15 dB
  RLS: +3.12 dB

Convergence speed (samples to within 1 dB of fixed baseline):
  LMS: 15 samples
  RLS: 2 samples

Sensitivity:
  LMS stable range: mu in [0.001, 0.010]
  RLS stable range: lambda in [0.95, 1.000]

=======================================================
  RECOMMENDATION FOR FUTURE HARDWARE IMPLEMENTATION
=======================================================

  For a stationary 5G NR channel with DMRS pilots:

  RECOMMENDED: LMS with mu = 0.001 to 0.005

  Rationale:
  - LMS achieves near-identical steady-state SINR to RLS
    (18.93 dB vs 18.90 dB, delta < 0.03 dB)
  - LMS converges in 15 samples -- well within 5G NR
    DMRS pilot length (~100-200 samples per slot)
  - LMS requires O(M) = 32 MACs/sample vs
    RLS O(M^2) = 288 MACs/sample -- 8x lower hardware cost
  - LMS maps directly to DSP48 pipeline (M parallel
    multiply-accumulate units, same structure as Phase 1)
  - RLS requires an MxM matrix inverse update -- expensive
    in hardware and requires M^2 registers

  RLS would be preferred if the channel is non-stationary
  (fast-moving user) requiring lambda < 1 for tracking.
  In a static lab scenario, LMS is sufficient.
